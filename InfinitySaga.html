PHASE 1

  • Iron Man: 8/10; I love this film! Super-rewatchable and cements Iron Man as the face of the Marvel Universe.
  • The Incredible Hulk: 5/10; This movie was alright - I've only ever watched it once because it's not even our Bruce Banner.
  • Iron Man 2: 7/10; This movie was good. Loved how Tony learned more about his father, ultimately saving his life: "My greatest creation is you." Also enjoyed Agent Coulson’s moments—I'm a big fan of Agents of Shield!
  • Thor: 6.5/10; This movie wasn’t bad. Thor grows a lot after being banished for his arrogance. And I don't think Loki is really a villain. He was lied to about where he comes from, lived in Thor’s shadow, and only ever wanted his father’s approval: ‘I could have done it, Father! For you! For all of us!’
  • Captain America The First Avenger : 8.5/10; I love this film but it is soooo sad. I love Steve and Bucky's friendship and hate that he didn't jump out at the end, he missed his date.
  • Avengers : 9/10; Spectacular all around. Love all the charcater dynamics but was devastated when Agent Coulson died (or did he?)

PHASE 2

  • Iron Man 3: 6.5/10; This film was good. I liked how we got to see more of who Tony is without the suit — his vulnerability, resourcefulness, and humanity really come through.
  • Thor The Dark World: 6/10; This movie was alright. The story with Jane didn’t capture my attention as much, and the villain felt a bit forgettable.
  • Captain America The Winter Soldier: 9/10; One of my absolute favorites. I love how this movie shows Cap trying to navigate a world that’s morally gray. The fight scenes are top-tier, and the emotional weight between Steve and Bucky makes it one of the strongest entries in the MCU.
  • Guardians of the Galaxy: 9/10; Another GREAT movie. I can re-watch this movie a thousand times - that's how good it is. Great storytelling, visuals, and characters.
  • Avengers Age of Ultron : 8/10; I don't understand why more people don't like this movie. I think it has a deep message and is beautifully captures human duality.
  • Ant Man: 8/10; I think Ant Man is so underated, this movie made me laugh, cry, and all of the above.

PHASE 3

  • Civil War: 9/10; This film was AMAZING! Team Iron Man all the way; I also loved the introduction of Spider-Man.
  • Doctor Strange: 7.5/10; This movie was good. I loved Dr. Strange's character development from once arrogant to now a powerful sorcerer.
  • Guardians of the Galaxy Volume 2: 8.5/10; I cried so much during this movie. Throughout the film, Quill often mentions being taken as a kid, but I love how Yondu genuinely protected him and how he ultimately found a real family.
  • Spider-Man Homecoming: 9/10; Spider-Man is my favorite superhero of all time. I don't know why people complain about Tom Holand's representation of Spider-Man. In this film, we see Peter truly find his own way.
  • Thor Ragnorak: 9.5/10; The BEST Thor movie to-date. It's funny, has great storytelling, and truly showcases who Thor is.
  • Black Panther: 9.5/10; This movie was a cinematic masterpiece! The visuals, the soundtrack, and the story all came together perfectly. I absolutely loved Killmonger — he’s one of the best villains in the MCU.
  • Avengers Infinity War: 10/10; JUST WOW! This film completely changed superhero movies by showing that heroes don’t always win. There are so many incredible moments, and I love how they gave each team their time to shine. Despite crying so much, I think it’s one of the best films in the MCU — intense, emotional, and unforgettable.
  • Ant Man and the Wasp: 6.5/10; This movie was good. I didn't think the villan, Ghost was too memorable but I do like how this film sets up Avengers Endgame.
  • Captain Marvel: 7/10; I don't hate Captain Marvel at all but, do think they just introduced her charcater too late. Although I do like her character dynamic with Nick Fury.
  • Avengers Endgame: 10/10; I can't even explain how good this movie is - I hate that I didn't get to experience it in theatres. I never liked the 5 year time jump but it makes sense. No one should be surprised when I say I bawled my eyes out when Iron Man died.
  • Spider-Man Far From Home: 8/10; This movie perfectly portrays Peter's grief and wanting to be a kid but at the end of the day knows he has a repsonsibility. I think Mysterio was a great villain and showcases how people believe anything.
